A structural engineer learning what the future of the profession looks like and preparing students for that future.

I worked in the construction industry for 10 years before becoming a lecturer on civil and structural engineering programmes. While in the industry, I spent half my time office based, working on design, mentoring graduates, and project managing etc, the other half I was on site doing safety and quality monitoring, and being client represenative etc. So I have seen both sides of the industry, granted all with the same large utility company.

I am a chartered engineer with Engineers Ireland, have an MSc in Structural Engineering from the University of Sheffield, a PGDip in Third Level Learning and Teaching in TU Dublin, and am now a PhD student at the University of Bristol. All while working full time, so I am use to part-time study at this stage but still struggle with the workload.

My PhD is looking at how embodied carbon of a structural project can be reduced. This is not purely a case of listing actions, this is an exercise in analysing how a whole system of people across the life of a project think. Every entity across the whole project process needs to recognise their contribution to the problem and make changes. From education to handover and everything in between. This research has benefits to me as an engineer, and as an educator of the next generation of structural engineers, but also to the industries community as a whole.

This blog will capture observations from my research and teaching experiences.
